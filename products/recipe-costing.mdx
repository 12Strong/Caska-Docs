---
title: Recipe Ingredients & Costing
description: Define what goes into each product using ingredients and packaging from your Inventory, and let Caska calculate your cost per unit automatically.
---

Caska lets you define what goes into each product — both recipe ingredients and packaging materials. This feeds into your cost-per-unit calculation so you know your actual production cost for every SKU.

## Adding recipe ingredients

On a product's detail page, find the **Recipe** section. You can add:

- **Ingredients** — raw materials from your Inventory. Select the inventory item and specify the quantity used per unit.
- **Packaging** — packaging materials also drawn from Inventory.

Linking ingredients to Inventory items means Caska can track stock depletion automatically when you fulfill orders.

## Cost per unit

Caska calculates cost per unit from the ingredient costs stored in your Inventory records. As you add recipe items, the estimated cost per unit updates automatically.

You can also enter a **manual cost override** if you prefer to track costing outside of the recipe system.

<Callout kind="tip">
  Make sure your Inventory items have accurate unit costs. The cost-per-unit calculation is only as good as the costs you've entered on each ingredient.
</Callout>

## Why this matters

Accurate costing gives you a real margin figure on every product. In Reports, you can compare revenue against production cost to see true profitability — not just revenue.
