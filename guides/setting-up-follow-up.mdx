title: Setting Up a Follow-Up Campaign
description: A walkthrough of configuring Caska's automated re-engagement system — from setting email sequences and intervals to monitoring the Follow-Up dashboard.
---

This guide walks you through setting up Caska's automated Follow-Up system so customers who haven't ordered in a while receive re-engagement emails on a schedule you control.

## How it works

Caska monitors each customer's last order date. When enough time has passed (as defined by your settings), it queues a follow-up email. If the customer still hasn't ordered, additional follow-ups continue on your defined schedule until they re-order or you manually remove them from the queue.

## Before you start

You'll need outgoing email configured in Caska before any emails can send. If you haven't set this up yet, see [Communication Settings](/settings/communication).

## Setting up the sequence

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings → Communication" titleType="p">
    Find the Communication section in your account settings. Follow-up sequences are turned off by default and turning them on will show additional settings below.
  </Step>

<Step title="Configure the schedule" titleType="p">
  Set **Days since last order** — this is how many days of inactivity before the
  first follow-up email sends. For example, 30 days means any customer who
  hasn't ordered in 30 days enters the queue.
</Step>

<Step title="Set the follow-up intervals" titleType="p">
  Define when subsequent emails send in the sequence and how many emails to send
  in total. The system works through the sequence in order until the customer
  re-orders or you reset them.
</Step>

    <Step title="Set the email timing and amount" titleType="p">
    Define how many follow up emails will send each day and what time of day they will send.

  </Step>
    
    <Step title="Configure the sending settings" titleType="p">
    Choose whether Caska will send the emails on your behalf (has a higher chance of being seen as spam) or connect to an email address you own and Caska will use it to send (comes directly from you.) If sending from an email address you own, enter the connection details.
  </Step>

<Step title="Create your email signature" titleType="p">
  Design your email signature to match what you use in your regular email.
</Step>

  <Step title="Save" titleType="p">
    The system activates and begins monitoring customer order activity.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Creating the Follow-up Email Messages

Go to **Settings → Communication → Follow-up Emails** to create sets of subject lines and email bodys to send to your customers. Caska will randomly choose one subject line and one email body to send, making sure that the messages mimic being sent directly from you.

Click on **+ADD SUBJECT** to add subject lines or click on the trash icon to remove existing options.

Click on **+ADD BODY** to add email body copy or click on the trash icon to remove existing options.

You can use {{customer_name}} and {{company_name}} as personalisation tokens in your messages, but use sparingly as there are no fallback values for these.

Click on **Save Templates** when you are done.

## Monitoring the queue and being notified of follow-ups going out

Go to **Follow-Up** in the main navigation (Owners and Admins only) to see:

- Which customers have had emails sent to them
- Which follow-up number they're on

Each day an email will be sent to the account owner outlining who has received follow-up emails that day and the content of those emails so you can keep track of the re-engagement emails being sent on your behalf.

## Managing opt-outs

If a specific customer should never receive follow-up emails, edit their customer record to uncheck the "Allow follow-up contact". If unchecked, no follow up emails will be sent to the customer.

<Callout kind="info">
  Follow-up emails should be configured to use your SMTP email settings. This
  will ensure that they are alwasy delivered. Emails sent by Caska's own email
  system have a much higher chance of being sent to a spam or junk folder.
</Callout>
