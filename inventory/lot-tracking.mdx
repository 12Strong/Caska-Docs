---
title: Lot Tracking
description: Record raw material lot numbers and expiry dates in Caska for food safety compliance, supplier traceability, and recall readiness.
---

Lot tracking lets you record which specific batch of raw material you're using — including lot numbers and expiry dates. This is important for food safety compliance, recall traceability, and quality control.

## Adding a lot to an inventory item

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open an inventory item" titleType="p">
    Click any item in the Inventory list to open its detail page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the Lots section and click Add Lot" titleType="p">
    Scroll to the **Lots** section and click **Add Lot**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter the lot details" titleType="p">
    - **Lot number** — from your supplier's label or your own internal numbering
    - **Quantity received**
    - **Expiry date** *(optional)*
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save" titleType="p">
    The lot is now recorded against this inventory item.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why lot tracking matters

If a raw material supplier issues a recall, you can quickly identify which finished products were made using that lot — critical for food businesses in retail channels.

## Expiry dates

Lots with expiry dates are visible in the lot list.

<Callout kind="alert">
  Caska does not currently auto-flag expired lots. Review your lot list periodically against current dates to catch any expired materials.
</Callout>
