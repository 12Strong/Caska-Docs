---
title: How the Follow-Up System Works
description: Automate re-engagement outreach to customers who haven't ordered recently using Caska's built-in follow-up email sequences.
---

The Follow-Up module automates outreach to customers who haven't ordered in a while. It sends a sequence of reminder emails on a schedule you define — so staying in front of your accounts doesn't rely on memory.

## The concept

Each customer has a **re-engagement date** — calculated from their last order. When that date passes, Caska queues a follow-up email and continues sending on your defined schedule until the customer re-orders or you manually reset them.

## Setting up the system

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings → Communication" titleType="p">
    Find the Communication section in your account settings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure re-engagement settings" titleType="p">
    - **Days since last order** before the first follow-up triggers
    - **Follow-up intervals** — e.g. first email at 30 days, second at 60, third at 90
    - **Email subject and body** for each follow-up in the sequence
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm your SMTP is configured" titleType="p">
    You need outgoing email configured under **Settings → Communication** for emails to send. See [Communication Settings](/settings/communication).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save" titleType="p">
    The system activates and begins monitoring customer order dates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The Follow-Up dashboard

The **Follow-Up** section in the navigation (Owners and Admins only) shows:

- Customers currently in the re-engagement queue
- Which follow-up number each customer is on
- When their next email is scheduled

From here you can manually remove customers from the queue or mark them as re-engaged.

## Opting customers out

Exclude a customer from follow-up emails by removing them from the queue in the Follow-Up dashboard or by updating their engagement status to an excluded status.

<Callout kind="info">
  Re-engagement emails are sent via Caska's system email (Mailgun), not your custom SMTP — even if you've configured a custom sending address. This ensures reliable delivery for automated sequences.
</Callout>
